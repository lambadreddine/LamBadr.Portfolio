I’m an **Industrial Maintenance Engineer** and **PhD Researcher** specializing in rotating machinery and predictive maintenance. My work sits at the intersection of heavy industry and artificial intelligence: I transform vibration signals and operational data into actionable maintenance strategies that prevent failures, reduce downtime, and lower costs.

I’m currently completing my PhD at Larbi Ben M’hidi University, where my research focuses on **AI-driven fault detection and advanced signal processing** (FFT, wavelets, machine learning). I build automated diagnostic tools that detect structural issues long before they escalate — turning raw sensor streams into reliability decisions.

Before my PhD, I spent years in hands‑on maintenance roles, diagnosing complex electromechanical faults in industrial machines and ensuring 100% operational readiness. That practical foundation now drives all my research: every algorithm I develop is designed to work in the real world, not just in a paper.

This portfolio showcases my projects, publications, code, and the blend of science and engineering that I bring to asset reliability.
